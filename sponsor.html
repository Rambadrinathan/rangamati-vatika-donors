How It Works

What Sponsorship Means Here

Every sponsorship gives you two things: Naming Rights and Nomination Rights.

⛭

Naming Rights

The unit, bed, or facility is permanently named after whoever you choose — yourself, a parent, a teacher, a departed loved one, a saint. The name appears on a hand-crafted plaque at the entrance, in English, Hindi and Bengali.

A son honouring his father. A family remembering their mother. A community celebrating a teacher. This is not a transaction — it is an act of devotion or remembrance.

☗

Nomination Rights

For residential units, the sponsor can nominate who the participant will be. A sponsor may or may not be a participant themselves — they can sponsor for someone else entirely.

Critical distinction: A sponsor contributes money and gets naming rights. A participant is the person who lives at the campus. These may or may not be the same person.

Every Category, In Detail

Gunagrahi Awaas

Residential Dormitory for GunaKul Learners

₹51 Lakhs

Each Gunagrahi Awaas houses 36 young learners in the GunaKul programme — the heart of the intergenerational promise. Children learn alongside elders in a biophilic campus designed for daily practice, study, and growth.

  • Naming rights on entrance plaque in English, Hindi and Bengali
  • 3 per ashram, 12 available across campus
  • First right of refusal to sponsor individual beds (12 beds at ₹2L each)
  • Full bed sponsorship add-on: ₹24 Lakhs additional (all 12 beds)
  • Total for Awaas + all beds: ₹75 Lakhs

Example: “Mata Ananya Devi Gunagrahi Awaas”

Swajan Griha

Private Elder Residence

₹21 Lakhs

A dignified private residence for elders on the banks of the Ganges. Each Swajan Griha is a home designed for lifetime community living — surrounded by purpose, youth, and the sacred river.

  • Naming rights on entrance plaque, permanent in English, Hindi and Bengali
  • 7 per ashram, 28 available across campus
  • Mandatory ₹4 Lakh payment by participant (Swajan) for living rights
  • If the Swajan chooses not to take back the ₹4L, the bed is permanently named after them — future participants never pay again
  • 2 beds per Griha, each separately sponsorable at ₹4 Lakhs
  • Full Swajan package (Griha + both beds): ₹29 Lakhs

Example: “Pitrideb Aditya Swajan Griha”

Common Questions

Frequently Asked

Can I sponsor a space in memory of someone who has passed?
Absolutely. Many sponsors choose to dedicate a space in loving memory of a parent, elder, or mentor. The dedication plaque will bear any name and honorific you choose. This is one of the most common and meaningful forms of sponsorship.
Can I visit the campus before sponsoring?
Yes. We welcome potential sponsors to visit RangaMati and see the land, the river, and the construction progress firsthand. Contact us to arrange a visit, and we will host you personally.
Is this a real estate investment?
No. This is a philanthropic sponsorship with naming rights, not a property purchase. You are sponsoring the construction of a community space, and your name will be permanently associated with it. The campus is owned and operated by KarmYog for 21st Century Foundation, an AOP registered in 2008 under the West Bengal Society Registration Act (XXVI), 1961.
What if I want to sponsor an entire ashram?
Each of the four ashrams can carry a single sponsor's name at the entrance, etched in stone in English, Hindi and Bengali. For ashram-level sponsorship, please speak with us directly. This is our highest tier and comes with the full Founder's Circle privileges plus additional recognition.
Can I nominate someone for residence even if I'm not a Swajan Griha sponsor?
Nomination rights for elder residence are specific to Swajan Griha sponsors. However, the campus welcomes applications from elders in need of dignified community living, and we will work to accommodate deserving individuals regardless of sponsorship status.
How long does the naming last?
Permanently. The dedication plaques are part of the building structure itself, designed to endure for generations. Your name is not on a temporary donor board, it is etched into the entrance of the space you sponsored.

Please Read Carefully

Important Information

What This Is & What This Is Not

  1. This is not a real estate transaction. Sponsorship does not confer ownership of land, buildings, or any immovable property.
  2. No land ownership is implied or granted. The campus land is held by the founding family; KYF has development and operating rights.
  3. There are no guaranteed financial returns. This is a sponsorship contribution, not an investment or purchase price.
  4. Rights are contractual and institutional — governed by the Sponsor Agreement between the sponsor and KYF / RV Trust.
  5. Monthly charges for residents are separate from the sponsorship amount. Residents (or their sponsors) pay ongoing living costs independently.
  6. Exit terms for Swajan Griha: up to 50% refund of the sponsorship amount on exit or demise of the participant, subject to replacement onboarding by the Trust.
  7. KYF and the RangaMati Vatika Trust retain full operational control of all campus spaces, programmes, and admissions.
