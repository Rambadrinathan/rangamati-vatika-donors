The Vision

Not a Real Estate Project. A Living Ecosystem.

KarmYog Vatika at RangaMati is a mission-led intergenerational institutional campus on the banks of the Ganges. It is not a housing society, not a retirement home, not a school. It is all of these woven into one — an ecosystem where the young learn from the old, the old find purpose in the young, and the principles of KarmYog come alive in daily practice.

The campus is spread across approximately 97 kathas of riverside land, with direct Ganges frontage. At its heart lies the Dharma Mata Shakti Devi Temple Complex, occupying roughly 17 kathas. Surrounding the temple, four identical Named Ashrams — each a self-contained community — occupy the remaining 80 kathas.

Construction of the Temple Complex commenced in January 2026. The land remains under the stewardship of the founding family, with KarmYog for 21st Century Foundation (KYF) holding development and operating rights.

Design Philosophy

Biophilic by Design, Not by Decoration

Every structure on the campus is designed around biophilic principles — green roofs, living walls, native flora, water channels, and natural light as primary design elements. This is not ornamentation. The architecture itself is a teaching tool.

Ganges-side meditation decks, farm-to-table community gardens, and learning spaces woven into the landscape ensure that nature is not something you visit — it is where you live.

The biophilic approach draws from over a decade of KY21C's work in ecological design, most recently demonstrated at the Biophilic Durga Puja 2025 at IIT Kharagpur Research Park — a 15,000 sq ft pavilion that drew 1 lakh visitors and 3 million digital views.

The Model

Two Generations, One Campus

The campus brings together two groups who need each other: Gunagrahis (learners) and Swajans (elders). The learners are young scholars enrolled in the KarmYog GunaKul, living in residential dormitories. The elders are those who have given a lifetime of service and now live in dignified community homes by the river.

They share dining halls, learning gardens, performing arts spaces, and daily rituals. The elders are not passive residents — they are mentors, storytellers, and living repositories of knowledge. The learners are not passive students — they bring energy, curiosity, and digital fluency.

This is not charity. It is an institutional model designed for mutual enrichment, sustained by a cross-subsidy structure: 40% self-paying, 30% contributing, and 30% scholarship-supported participants.
